# Stage 7: Critical Path

## Purpose
Map the complete journey from "user has the problem" to "user's problem is
solved" — every step in between. This is NOT about app navigation or screens
(that's Stage 9, App Structure). This is about the EXPERIENCE: awareness,
discovery, first use, magic moment, and what keeps them coming back.

## Emotional Arc
Clarity, momentum. "Here's the whole journey..." The user should see their
product as a complete experience, not just a collection of features.

## Conversation Flow

### Step 1: Map the journey together
Start from the very beginning — before the user even knows about the product:

> "Let's trace the whole path. Someone out there has [problem] right now.
> Walk me through everything that happens from that moment to the moment
> they're a happy, regular user of your product."

Prompt through each phase:
- **Awareness:** "How do they find out this exists? What triggers them to look?"
- **Discovery:** "They land on your product. What do they see? What makes them
  try it instead of closing the tab?"
- **Onboarding:** "First time opening it. What happens? How fast do they get
  to the good part?"
- **Magic moment:** "This is where they go 'oh wow.' We defined this in Stage 4
  as [reference]. How many steps between opening the app and this moment?"
- **Habit formation:** "They had the magic moment. What brings them back tomorrow?
  Next week? What's the loop?"
- **Expansion:** "They're hooked. What makes them tell someone else about it?
  What makes them upgrade/pay/invest more?"

### Step 2: Find the friction points
For each transition in the journey:
> "Where could someone drop off? What's the weakest link in this chain?"

Common friction points:
- Too many steps between download and magic moment
- Onboarding asks for too much before delivering value
- No clear trigger to come back after first use
- Magic moment requires other people (network effects) but user is alone

### Step 3: Tighten the critical path
For each friction point:
> "How do we remove this friction? Can we get to the magic moment in fewer steps?"

The goal: the shortest possible path from "has the problem" to "problem solved."

## Assumption Tracking
"Users will discover this through [channel]" and "Users will come back because [reason]"
are both critical assumptions. Flag them.

## Section Output Format

```markdown
## 7. Critical Path

**The journey:**

1. **Has the problem** — [What the user is experiencing/feeling]
2. **Discovers the product** — [How they find it, what triggers the search]
3. **First impression** — [What they see, what makes them try it]
4. **Onboarding** — [Steps to get started, what's required vs optional]
5. **Magic moment** — [The "oh wow" — reference from Stage 4]
6. **First value** — [First time the problem is actually solved]
7. **Return trigger** — [What brings them back tomorrow]
8. **Habit loop** — [The repeating cycle that makes it stick]
9. **Expansion** — [What makes them tell others or invest more]

**Steps from download to magic moment:** [number]

**Biggest friction point:** [Where users are most likely to drop off]

**How we address it:** [Solution or design decision]

**Retention hook:** [What keeps them coming back]

### Discussion Notes
[How the path evolved, friction points found, shortcuts discovered]
```
